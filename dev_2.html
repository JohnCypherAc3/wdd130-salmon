Tips and Tricks

Armor

Choosing the right armor is one of the most important parts of a true melee playthrough. Most melee-focused armor sets provide high defense, which is essential since you must stay close to enemies to deal damage. Higher defense allows you to survive longer and gives you more opportunities to attack safely. Prioritize armor that boosts melee damage and survivability rather than mobility alone.

Accessories

Accessories can greatly improve your effectiveness in combat. Reforging accessories is key.

  • Increased defense
  • Damage reduction
  • Higher damage output
  • Critical strike chance
  • Increased maximum health

Balancing survivability and damage is crucial. Some accessories, such as the Hide of Astrum Deus, are especially valuable because they directly enhance true melee strikes and can make a major difference in difficult boss fights.

Weapons

True melee weapons generally have very short range, so positioning is everything. Learning how to move in and out of enemy range safely is essential.

Reforging weapons can improve performance by increasing size, damage, or speed. Even small increases can help you land more hits while avoiding damage.

Bosses and Enemies

Boss fights are the biggest challenge for true melee players. Many bosses rely on projectiles and try to stay out of range, making consistent damage difficult.

A strong strategy is to move in quickly, deal damage, then back off to dodge attacks. Repeat this pattern when it is safe.

This class performs especially well against groups of enemies and worm-type bosses, since each swing can hit multiple segments for high damage.

Mining

Mining may seem unrelated to combat, but it can help in dangerous situations. Most pickaxes deal true melee damage, meaning they benefit from your melee boosts.

This allows you to defend yourself while mining without needing to switch weapons.

Final Tip

True melee is all about risk and reward. You deal high damage, but only when you are close to danger. Learning enemy patterns, improving movement, and staying patient will make the challenge much more manageable and rewarding.
